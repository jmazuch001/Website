About Me

I have been learning web and software development for the last two and a half years, learning everything from front-end to back-end development. I am ready to begin looking for opportunities in web development, very flexible for starting out and open to almost any location! I have a diverse enducational background and have worked on various front-end projects and have no problem using all of my skills to accomplish a task. My projects are available for review on my GitHub, as well as my portfolio page to this website. I have made it easy to access my GitHub by clicking on my assortment of projects, as well as a link in the navigation bar.

I left manufacturing in good graces and started in corrections, where I would ultimately be able to fund all of my learning in web development. I had considered going into cybersecurity, but believe that I'd rather engineer other solutions for the time being. While in corrections, I was welcomed into a family of officers, realizing that we had each other's back. It's undeniable that it was a formidable environment, but sticking to policy was often the best method and it taught me the value of having clarity in certain areas where one's own judgement wasn't appropriate. The biggest impact it had on me was gaining the respect of not only my co-workers, but also that of the inmate population. I used motivational psychology and a professional, objective approach whenever possible and it turned out to be a personal policy I'd come to enforce, too. In the meantime, it gave me time to spend with my parents after being away for college for almost eight years.

I am an alarmingly fast learner, and, not limited to my Bachelor of Science in Psychology, I first started learning web development at the beginning of 2017 using TeamTreehouse, completing the "How to Make A Basic Web Page" track, and diving deeper into the individual technologies henceforth. It really helped shape my understanding of web technologies throughout, including my own usage of Bootstrap. Last year, I was awarded the Grow With Google Front-End Developer Scholarship through Google and Udacity. Growing up around computers and having an eye for art and detail also helps me exercise my creativity, allowing me to create a UX-oriented, aesthetically pleasing, responsive design. Since then, I have added HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap 3, Bootstrap 4, JavaScript, jQuery, Node.js, JSON, Git/Git Bash, Python and MySQL to my repertoire (plus many, many others) and am eager to get to work! I also have a number of apps hosted on Heroku (link will be provided upon request).

I enjoy figuring things out on my own, but realize that there can be time constraints. I'm the type of hard worker that will have the tendency to do research in my off-time after work, come back the next day and solve the problem - or I'll ask for input. The importance of production and churning out quality results is highly important to me, as I don't like others to be waiting on me. Given my passion for working with technology, I am quite confident that my skills can best aid in a Web Developer. I am eager to start showing what I've got and giving back to others who are making their way, helping out in the community or at University. In all of my job history, I am proud to say that my reliability, work ethic, and dedication always show a strong trend, and am fortunate to have heard so from supervisors. If you have any questions, please feel free to use the means of contact which I have provided throughout my website.
